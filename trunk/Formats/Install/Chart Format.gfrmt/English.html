Chart Format

This format is designed for printing charts of individuals, families, research logs, and notes. These charts provide a concise way to document basic information on your genealogy records. This format has only minimal editing capabilities (for birth, death, and marriage dates and places). For records not support with a chart option (e.g. multimedia and sources), this format has no editing or display functions.

Records Display

Individual Records

Individuals are displayed as a three generation pedigree chart. This chart shows the individual and their spouses, parents, grand parents, and great grandparents. Each ancestor lists their vital information. The main individual in the chart has fields for editing the vital information.

You can click any name to open that person's pedigree chart. To navigate to children, use the "Children" command in the "View" menu (or click the children icon in the tool bar). To view families with spouses, click the "Family" links under the spouse's name.

The chart will resize as you resize the window, but when printing, it will be fit to a single page. If you resize wider than a page, a red line will appear at the current page boundary. If you resize until the red line just disappears, you will see how the chart will print. When printing, the editing fields will display as simple text rather than framed fields. If the chart is to too cramped in display or when printing, use the "Page Setup..." command to decrease the print margins.

Family Records

Families display as a chart that shows the husband and wife, their marriage details, and each child in the family. The chart width is equal to the current printing page width. It will print to one or more pages as a summary of that family.

You can click any name in the chart to open that person's pedigree chart. To navigate to families for the children, use the "Families" command in the "View" menu (or click the families icon in the tool bar).

Research Log Records

Research log records will display and print as a complete display of the information in the log. These charts are useful for printing when traveling (e.g. to a family history library) and you would like a hard copy of research plans. You can click the "Edit" button to edit the summaries in the research log.

Notes Records

Notes records will display and print a title and the complete text of the notes. You can click the "Edit" button to edit the notes.

Chart Format Preferences

  • Fonts - the specific display fonts are listed below. If these are made too large, the chart may not print well on a single page.
    1. Name Text - font for displaying names (which are links to records as well).
    2. Editing Text - font used for fields that allow editing.
    3. Notes Editing Text - font used for editing text in notes and research log records.
    4. Notes Text - font for display of notes and research log summaries.
    5. Details Text - font for vital information displayed in pedigree charts and details listed in family records.
    6. Label Text - font for some labels.
    7. Static Text - font for some secondary labels.
    8. Popup Menu Text - font used in popup menus (only in research logs).
    9. Button Text - font for a few buttons.
  • Colors - the "Editing Background" and "Window Background" are recommended to be left as white for better printing.
  • Editing - Chart Format Settings
    1. Individual Label Width - width of labels next to editing fields in pedigree charts.
    2. Individual Label Offset - offset of labels next to editing fields to align well with the editing text.

Chart Format Languages

To choose the language for this format, open the "Editing" tab of theGEDitCOM II preferences and select a language from the pop-up menu. The options are:

  • English
  • French

These settings change the langauge in the record browsing windows, but do not change other elements of GEDitCOM II such as menus, sheets, and documentation.
